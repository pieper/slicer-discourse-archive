<ul>
<li>
<p><a class="mention" href="/u/pieper">@pieper</a> spoke with the <strong>nnInteractive</strong> team:</p>
<ul>
<li>Current models include training data licensed under <strong>non-commercial Creative Commons</strong>, restricting their use in commercial settings.</li>

</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><a name="p-126880-using-nninteractive-to-refine-totalsegmentator-output-10" class="anchor" href="#p-126880-using-nninteractive-to-refine-totalsegmentator-output-10" aria-label="Heading link"></a>Using <code>nnInteractive</code> to Refine TotalSegmentator Output</h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p><a class="mention" href="/u/pieper">@pieper</a> tested whether <code>nnInteractive</code> can be used to refine <strong>rough or low-resolution TotalSegmentator segmentations</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Feedback from <strong>Fabian Isensee</strong>  (lead author of <code>nnInteractive</code>) was positive, though this use case hadn’t been formally tested.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Results:</p>
<ul>
<li>Left: Native TotalSegmentator output at <strong>1.5mm resolution</strong> (from input with <strong>3.75mm slice spacing</strong>).</li>
<li>Right: After upsampling the labelmap and refining using <code>nnInteractive</code>, improved results were obtained by selecting a segment and adding just <strong>one point</strong>.</li>
</ul>
