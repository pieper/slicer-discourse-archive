## Post #1 by @fedorov (2022-04-06 17:40 UTC)

<p><a href="https://imaging.datacommons.cancer.gov/">NCI Imaging Data Commons</a> April 2022 release is out!</p>
<p>Here are some of the highlights:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://learn.canceridc.dev/data/data-release-notes#v8-april-2022">Data</a>: slide microscopy imaging data is now available for <em>all</em> of the TCGA collections and for the NLST collection - that’s the total of &gt;14 TB of new slide microscopy data (if you are curious how to get to this number, check out <a href="https://gist.github.com/fedorov/a956b59eefb1f201477e5812e3817813">this query</a>)</li>
<li><a href="https://learn.canceridc.dev/portal/release-notes#8.0-april-4-2022-canceridc.202204050856.2920c81">Portal</a>: you can now choose whether you want to search the collections that are no longer available in IDC, and they are now excluded by default; we also excluded those collections from the overall statistics on the front page, which resulted in the reduction in data summary numbers relative to v7</li>
<li><a href="https://learn.canceridc.dev/portal/viewer-release-notes#4.12.17-february-2022-ohif">Viewers</a>: we added new features for quantitative microscopy imaging and several performance enhancements, including client-side decoding and color correction of compressed images using WebAssembly as well as transformation and rendering of images using WebGL</li>
</ul>
