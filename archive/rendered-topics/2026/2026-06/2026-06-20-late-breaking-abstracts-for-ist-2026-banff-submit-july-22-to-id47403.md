## Post #1 by @Graham_Little (2026-06-20 21:38 UTC)

<p>Dear colleagues,</p>
<p>Registration for IST 2026 in Banff is open, and we are adding a late-breaking abstract window for work that wasn’t ready by the spring deadline. If you have new results, this is a second route onto the program.</p>
<p><strong>Conference dates:</strong> October 25 to 29, 2026<br>
<strong>Location:</strong> Banff Centre for Arts and Creativity, Banff National Park, Canada<br>
<strong>Late-breaking window:</strong> July 22 to August 19, 2026<br>
<strong>Submit here:</strong> <a href="https://event.fourwaves.com/ist2026" class="inline-onebox" rel="noopener nofollow ugc">Event Website - Fourwaves</a></p>
<p>IST 2026 is the society’s second annual meeting. It runs across the full tractography workflow, from anatomy and validation through methods and clinical applications, with time set aside for direct conversation with the people using and building the tools.</p>
<p>What’s on the program:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Keynotes from Neda Jahanshad (Keck School of Medicine, USC; ENIGMA), Luis Concha (Institute of Neurobiology, UNAM), and Forrest Collman (Allen Institute for Brain Science)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Masterclasses in three tracks: Anatomy and Validation, Analysis and Methods, and Clinical Applications</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A program where every trainee presents, with a power pitch and a poster</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The window closes August 19, and there are fewer late-breaking slots than in the main round, so send yours in early once submissions open.</p>
<p>Best regards,<br>
The IST 2026 Organizing Committee</p>
