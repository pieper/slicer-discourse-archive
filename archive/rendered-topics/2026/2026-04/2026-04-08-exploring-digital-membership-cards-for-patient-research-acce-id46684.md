## Post #1 by @hanblue (2026-04-08 14:14 UTC)

<p>Hi everyone,</p>
<p>I’ve been thinking about how digital identity and access systems can be improved in medical imaging and research environments, especially in setups where multiple institutions collaborate.</p>
<p>In many industries, emembership cards are used to manage user access across different locations or services. I’m curious if a similar concept could be applied in healthcare or medical imaging workflows.</p>
<p>For example, a digital membership card (or digital ID) could potentially be used for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Managing patient access across multiple diagnostic centers</li>
<li>Allowing researchers to securely access shared imaging datasets</li>
<li>Providing role-based access (doctors, technicians, external collaborators)</li>
<li>Tracking usage of imaging tools and resources across institutions</li>
</ul>
<p>In environments where tools like 3D Slicer are used for imaging and analysis, I was wondering:</p>
<ol>
<li>Are there any existing approaches to integrate user identity or access control with imaging workflows?</li>
<li>How do organizations currently handle cross-institution access to imaging data securely?</li>
<li>Could a <a href="https://membershipanywhere.com/digital-membership/" rel="noopener nofollow ugc">digital membership card</a> system (QR-based or app-based) be integrated with existing medical imaging infrastructure?</li>
<li>Are there any modules, APIs, or external tools that could support such a system alongside Slicer?</li>
</ol>
<p>The goal would be to create a more seamless and secure way to manage access without relying heavily on manual verification or fragmented systems.</p>
<p>I’d really appreciate any insights, real-world implementations, or ideas on how something like this could fit into current medical imaging ecosystems.</p>
